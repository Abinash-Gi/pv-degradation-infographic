PV Degradation & Cost Recovery in Germany

Key Insights for 1-100 kWp Systems

Based on research by Abinash Satapathy, Hochschule Ansbach

The Challenge: Solar Panel Degradation

Photovoltaic (PV) panels lose efficiency over time. This degradation impacts the financial viability of solar investments by reducing energy output and extending payback periods.

This study focuses on 1-100 kWp systems in Germany, a crucial segment for distributed energy.

Key Findings

Degradation's Toll (at 0.7% annually)

Payback Period: Extended by 0.05 - 0.12 years.

Annualized ROI: Reduced by 0.2 - 0.4 percentage points.

LCOE: Increased by €0.005 - €0.008 /kWh.

Lifetime Energy Yield: Reduced by ~8%.

Even moderate degradation visibly erodes financial returns over 25 years.

Economies of Scale Shine

Larger Systems (100 kWp) vs. Smaller (5 kWp):

Baseline Payback: 3.93 years (100kWp) vs. 6.08 years (5kWp).

Baseline Ann. ROI: 25.42% (100kWp) vs. 16.45% (5kWp).

Baseline LCOE: €0.059/kWh (100kWp) vs. €0.089/kWh (5kWp).

Bigger is generally better for PV economics due to lower per-kWp costs.

Self-Consumption is King (100 kWp Example)

100% Self-Consumption

Baseline Payback: 3.93 years

Baseline Ann. ROI: 25.42%

50/50 Self-Consumption/Feed-In (FIT €0.05/kWh)

Baseline Payback: 7.00 years

Baseline Ann. ROI: 14.29%

Maximizing on-site energy use is substantially more profitable than exporting to the grid at low FIT rates.

The Bottom Line

PV investments in Germany (5-100 kWp) remain financially viable, especially with high self-consumption.

However, degradation is a critical factor that erodes returns. Quality components and accurate performance modeling are essential.

These quantified insights are crucial for homeowners, businesses, and policymakers in Germany's Energiewende.
